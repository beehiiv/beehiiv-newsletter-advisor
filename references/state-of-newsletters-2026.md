## Platform-Wide Benchmarks (2024 vs 2025)

| Metric | 2024 | 2025 |
|---|---|---|
| Delivery Rate | 98.31% | **98.90%** |
| Open Rate | 37.67% | **41.24%** |
| Click-Through Rate | 4.74% | **3.23%** |
| Bounce Rate | 1.64% | **1.48%** |
| Spam Complaint Rate | 0.04% | **0.02%** |

> Note on open rates: Apple's Mail Privacy Protection (MPP) auto-loads images, inflating traditional open metrics. beehiiv proactively filters bot activity for more accurate data. Industry is shifting toward "true engagement" metrics: verified clicks, replies, and post-email actions.

> Note on CTR decline: CTR is down because readers increasingly consume content *within* the email itself (especially for high-quality newsletters) rather than clicking out. The bar for compelling CTAs is rising.

## Send Timing Data

### Best Days (highest engagement)
- Tuesdays and Wednesdays outperform other days

### Best Times of Day
- **Peak 1: Early Morning** (strongest overall — readers check email first thing)
- **Peak 2: Mid-afternoon** (secondary bump)
- **Worst window: Overnight** (lowest engagement across the board)

### Send Frequency
Most successful creators send **1–3x per week**. Consistent cadence matters more than volume.

## Subject Line Engagement

- **Short subject lines drive more opens** — keep them concise
- Question formats and curiosity gaps perform well
- Avoid spam-trigger words and excessive punctuation
- Personalization in subject lines (first name) modestly lifts opens

## Deliverability & List Hygiene (2025 Insights)

- Gmail, Outlook, Yahoo all strengthened enforcement favoring senders with **engaged audiences**
- Inactive subscriber segments actively hurt deliverability — **engagement is the new deliverability firewall**
- Best practices from TIME's email team: remove bad addresses, maintain consistent send schedule, expand welcome + re-engagement automations
- Interactive elements (polls, quizzes, surveys) boost sender reputation with ISPs — not just surface engagement
- beehiiv's Verified Clicks feature filters bot/fraud clicks for cleaner metrics

## 5 Key Trends for 2026

1. **Newsletters → multi-channel media hubs.** Top publishers (TIME, Half Baked, Garbage Day) are adding events, podcasts, community, and companion formats. The newsletter is the hub, not the whole product.

2. **Growth leaders prioritize list hygiene over list size.** Sending to 10K highly-engaged readers beats sending to 50K unengaged ones. Deliverability and monetization both depend on this.

3. **IRL events are a major opportunity.** In-person community events are a breakout monetization and growth lever for 2026.

4. **AI-driven personalization at scale.** Segmentation, dynamic content, and personalized sends are becoming table stakes, not differentiators.

5. **More aligned sponsorships.** Advertisers want niche relevance over raw reach. A 5K-subscriber finance newsletter can command higher CPMs than a 100K general newsletter.

## FAQ Answers (from the report)

**Are newsletters still growing in 2026?** Yes — 28B emails sent in 2025, 255M unique readers, open rates up to 41%+.

**What is a good open rate in 2026?** Platform average is 41.24%. 35%+ is healthy; 50%+ is exceptional (top niche categories).

**How often should creators send?** 1–3x per week. Consistency matters more than frequency.

**Are paid newsletters still profitable?** Yes — $19M in paid subscription revenue in 2025, up 138% YoY. Median 66 days to first dollar for new 2025 launches.
